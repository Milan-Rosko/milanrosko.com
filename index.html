I came to logic through design: the same questions about structure, compression, and legibility kept reappearing, only in a stricter language.

Most of my current work sits around first-order systems, constructive semantics, and the behavior of impredicativity without the full classical apparatus. I am interested in how much expressive strength remains when proofs are read under the BHK Interpretation.

In the mid-20th century, the cybernetic vision was ahead of its institutional time. Today, software mediates coordination, security is adversarial, and “proof” increasingly means verifiable procedure. In that environment, the intuitionist viewpoint is no longer only philosophical; it is effective because it asks what can actually be constructed, checked, and run. Currently enrolled at the University of Hagen's BA Computer Science.
