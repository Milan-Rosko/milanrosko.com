About me

I hold a B.A. in Gestaltung und Kultur from HTW Berlin University of Applied Sciences and am currently enrolled in the B.A. Computer Science program at FernUniversität in Hagen.

My work focuses on first-order systems, computability, and the behavior of impredicativity outside the full classical apparatus. I arrived at intuitionistic formal logic partly through design: questions of structure, compression, and legibility kept reappearing, but in a stricter and more explicit language.

Most importantly, as software mediates much of everyday life, security becomes increasingly adversarial, and “proof” comes to mean verifiable procedure, constructive semantics offers a useful lens, a reliableground for reasoning, and a form of stability: a way of keeping thought attached to witnesses, constructions, and decidable transitions.
